What Is Single Dose Grinding (and Why Does It Matter)?

Single dose grinding means loading only the beans you need for one shot — typically 16–20g — directly into the grinder before each pull. No hopper sitting full of coffee exposed to air and light.

The core benefits:

  • Freshness preserved: Coffee stales within days of grinding and within weeks of roasting. Grinding per-shot means every espresso uses the freshest possible grounds.
  • Zero waste: No leftover grinds sitting in a half-empty hopper between sessions.
  • Dialing flexibility: Switching between origins or roast levels is instant — no purging hundreds of grams to clear old beans.
  • Retention matters: A grinder that retains 1–2g per shot effectively wastes your best beans and introduces stale grounds into the next dose. In 2026, the benchmark for a quality single dose grinder is under 0.5g retention.

Key Metrics to Evaluate Before You Buy

Grind Retention

Retention is the weight difference between what you load and what you actually receive in the portafilter. Look for grinders under 0.5g retention. Most single dose designs use straight-through grinding paths or integrated bellows to minimize this.

Burr Type: Flat vs. Conical

  • Flat burrs (e.g., SSP 64mm, Etzinger 58mm) tend to produce a more uniform, defined particle size distribution — favored for clarity and brightness in espresso. They typically run at lower RPMs to reduce heat.
  • Conical burrs (e.g., Mazzer Kony, Niche's proprietary 63mm conical) produce a bimodal distribution with more fines, which many baristas find creates body and sweetness in the cup. They're also generally quieter and more forgiving of workflow variation.

Neither is objectively superior — it's a matter of taste and workflow preference.

RPM Speed

High-RPM grinding introduces heat, which can degrade volatile aromatic compounds mid-grind. For espresso, low-RPM motors (under 500 RPM for flat burr grinders) are widely considered preferable. High-RPM grinders (700 RPM+) sacrifice some cup quality for speed — a meaningful trade-off at the home barista level where shot-to-shot consistency matters more than throughput.

Stepless vs. Stepped Adjustment

Stepless grind adjustment gives you infinite micro-adjustment — critical for dialing in espresso, where tiny changes in grind size dramatically affect extraction. Stepped grinders restrict you to preset increments, which can be frustrating if your ideal setting falls between two clicks.

Essential Accessories for Single Dose Workflow

A great grinder performs better with the right accessories:

  • Bellows: Forces retained grounds through the chute after grinding. Essential for near-zero waste.
  • RDT spray bottle: The Ross Droplet Technique involves adding a single drop of water to beans pre-grind to reduce static and clumping. Dramatically improves grind distribution.
  • Anti-static dosing funnel: Prevents grounds from scattering during dosing.
  • Magnetic dosing cups: Compatible with several grinder platforms for seamless workflow.

FAQ: Common Single Dose Grinder Questions

Is single dose grinding actually worth it?

For home baristas who rotate between multiple coffees, drink one or two shots per day, or prioritize freshness above all else — yes, research and user consensus consistently support the value of single dose grinding. For those brewing larger volumes with a single bean, a traditional hopper setup may be more practical.

What's the best single dose grinder under $500?

Based on aggregated reviews and specifications as of 2026, the Niche Zero is the most consistently recommended option in this range for its near-zero retention, quiet operation, and reliable consistency.

Do I need a bellows attachment?

Not strictly necessary, but highly recommended. Even grinders marketed as "zero retention" typically retain 0.1–0.3g, and a bellows costs under $20 while meaningfully improving dosing accuracy.

What espresso machine pairs best with a single dose grinder?

Any machine with precise temperature control and pressure profiling will let a quality single dose grinder shine. Look for machines with PID temperature control as a baseline.

Can I use a single dose grinder for filter coffee too?

Many single dose grinders — especially those with wide grind range adjustment — double effectively as filter grinders. The Niche Zero and Lagom P64 are both widely used across brew methods.
