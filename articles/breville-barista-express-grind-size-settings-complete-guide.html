Understanding the Two Grind Adjustments

The Outer Dial: 16 Steps from Fine to Coarse

The Breville Barista Express features a 16-step outer grind size dial. Lower numbers (toward 1) produce a finer grind; higher numbers (toward 16) produce a coarser grind. For espresso, most users land somewhere between 5 and 8 as a starting point — but that number shifts based on your beans, roast level, and freshness.

This dial is the adjustment most owners know about. The one that's not always explicitly highlighted in the quick-start guide is the inner burr ring.

The Inner Burr Micro-Adjustment Ring

Removing the hopper reveals a second adjustment ring seated around the upper burr. This ring provides additional steps between each number on the outer dial, allowing for much finer calibration than the outer dial alone permits.

The number of micro-steps available varies by model. The BES870XL and BES878BSS both feature this inner ring, but the exact step count per position can differ slightly between production runs. Breville's official support documentation is the most reliable source for your specific unit — check the Breville support page and search your model number for the current manual.

General guidance: each click of the inner ring moves grind size by roughly one-ninth of a full outer-dial step, giving you extremely precise control once you're close to your target setting.

Step-by-Step Dialing-In Workflow

Follow this repeatable process each time you switch beans or notice shot quality drifting.

  1. Set your dose. For a double shot, use 18–22g of grounds in the portafilter. Weigh it — a precision espresso scale with timer and 0.1g resolution removes the guesswork entirely.
  2. Tamp evenly. Apply consistent, level pressure. A calibrated 58mm espresso tamper with tamping mat helps eliminate the variable of inconsistent tamping pressure.
  3. Pull the shot. Start your timer when you engage the pump. Aim for 25–35 seconds to yield approximately 36–40g of espresso from an 18–20g dose.
  4. Observe the flow. The stream should flow steadily — not drip slowly or gush fast. A naked/bottomless portafilter compatible with Breville 54mm basket makes channeling and uneven extraction visually obvious.
  5. Taste and evaluate. Note whether the shot is sour, balanced, or bitter (see Troubleshooting below).
  6. Adjust by one step at a time. Change only the grind size between shots. Moving multiple variables at once makes it impossible to know what caused the improvement.
  7. Repeat until the shot pulls in the target window and tastes balanced.

Troubleshooting by Shot Symptom

Shot Pulls Too Fast / Tastes Sour or Thin

  • Cause: Grind is too coarse; water passes through without adequate resistance.
  • Fix: Move the outer dial one step finer (lower number). If you're already at a low number, try the inner micro-adjustment ring first.

Shot Pulls Too Slow / Tastes Bitter or Harsh

  • Cause: Grind is too fine; excessive resistance leads to over-extraction.
  • Fix: Move the outer dial one step coarser (higher number).

Shot Channels or Sprays Unevenly

  • Cause: Often tamping inconsistency or an uneven grind distribution, not grind size itself.
  • Fix: Check your tamp technique and distribution before adjusting grind.

Shot Tastes Watery Despite Correct Timing

  • Cause: Dose may be too low, or the grind is slightly too coarse for the bean.
  • Fix: Verify your dose weight first, then adjust grind finer by one step.

Using specialty single-origin or espresso blend coffee beans roasted within the past three weeks gives you fresher, more predictable grounds to work with while dialing in.

Maintenance: Recalibrating After Cleaning or Burr Replacement

Cleaning the grinder — or replacing burrs — resets your baseline. Here's how to recalibrate:

  1. Run a purge dose (5–10g of beans) after reassembling to clear any cleaning residue and seat the burrs.
  2. Use burr grinder cleaning tablets and brush kit designed for espresso grinders; avoid blowing compressed air directly into the burr chamber.
  3. Start from scratch on the outer dial. Don't assume your previous setting is still valid — cleaning removes coffee oil buildup that was subtly affecting grind resistance.
  4. Reset the inner ring to its midpoint before running your first calibration shot.
  5. Pull three to four calibration shots using a known-good bean before drawing conclusions about the new setting.

Water quality also affects extraction consistency. Running Breville-compatible descaling solution and a water filtration pitcher on a regular maintenance schedule prevents mineral buildup that can alter shot timing independent of grind size.

Bean Storage Matters Too

Stale beans behave unpredictably in the grinder. A airtight coffee bean storage container with CO2 one-way valve extends freshness and keeps your dialing-in work from being undone by oxidation. Store at room temperature away from direct light.

FAQ: Common Breville Barista Express Grind Questions

What grind size for light roast on the Barista Express? Start between 3 and 5 on the outer dial. Light roasts are denser and less soluble, requiring a finer grind to achieve proper extraction in the 25–35 second window.

Why is my espresso watery on the Barista Express? A watery shot usually means under-extraction from a grind that's too coarse, an insufficient dose, or both. Check your dose weight first, then move the grind one step finer.

How often should I adjust the inner burr ring? Only when the outer dial alone can't get you to the right extraction time. Think of the inner ring as fine-tuning, not a primary adjustment.

Do I need to recalibrate when switching from dark to light roast? Yes — expect to move at least 2–3 steps finer when switching from a dark to a light roast.

Can I adjust the grind while the machine is grinding? Breville recommends adjusting the outer dial while the grinder is running for smooth movement. The inner ring should only be adjusted with the hopper removed and the machine off.
