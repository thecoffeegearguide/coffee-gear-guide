What Triggers the CLEAN ME Light?

The Breville Barista Express monitors usage and prompts descaling through its CLEAN ME alert. According to Breville's official documentation, the machine uses a brew-count-based system to trigger this alert — it tracks the number of brew cycles completed and factors in your programmed water hardness setting to calculate when descaling is due. It is not simply a fixed timer.

This is why setting your water hardness level correctly matters. Breville includes water hardness test strips in the box for exactly this reason.

How to Set Your Water Hardness Level

  1. Dip a test strip into a glass of your tap water for one second, then wait 60 seconds.
  2. Compare the strip color to the chart included with the strips.
  3. On the Barista Express, enter programming mode by pressing and holding the PROGRAM button for five seconds.
  4. Use the dial to select your hardness level (SOFT, MEDIUM, HARD, or VERY HARD).
  5. Press PROGRAM again to confirm and save.

Breville recommends descaling every 2–3 months in hard water areas and less frequently in soft water areas. Recalibrating this setting ensures the CLEAN ME light fires at the right interval for your water supply.

What You Need Before You Start

Gather the following before initiating the descaling cycle:

  • Descaling solution — Breville's official descaling solution packets or food-grade citric acid powder (roughly 1–2 tablespoons dissolved in 1.5 liters of water)
  • A container — Must hold at least 1.5 liters; place it under the steam wand and group head
  • Fresh water — For rinse cycles
  • A clean cloth — To wipe down the exterior and catch splatter

How to Descale the Breville Barista Express: Step-by-Step

Initiating the Descaling Cycle

The descaling mode on the Barista Express is not activated through the menu — it uses a specific button combination.

  1. Make sure the machine is on and in standby mode (not brewing).
  2. Empty the drip tray and water tank completely.
  3. Mix your descaling solution into the water tank per the product instructions (or dissolve citric acid in 1.5 liters of water).
  4. Place your large container under both the group head and the steam wand tip.
  5. Press and hold the 1-cup and 2-cup buttons simultaneously for approximately three seconds until the CLEAN ME light begins to flash — this activates descaling mode.

Note: Button combinations may vary slightly between firmware versions. Consult your specific model's manual if this sequence does not respond as expected.

Running the Descaling Cycle

  1. Press the POWER button (or the button indicated on your display) to begin the first descaling flush. The machine will dispense solution in intervals — this is normal.
  2. Allow the full first flush to complete. The machine will pause automatically between intervals.
  3. When prompted (the indicator light will change), empty your container and refill the water tank with fresh, clean water only — no solution.
  4. Press the button again to begin the first rinse cycle.
  5. Repeat the rinse process a second time if your machine prompts it — some cycles include two rinse flushes.
  6. The CLEAN ME light will turn off once the cycle completes successfully, indicating descaling is finished.

The full process typically takes 25–35 minutes from start to finish, including pauses between intervals.

After Descaling: Final Cleanup Steps

  • Remove, rinse, and dry the water tank thoroughly before refilling with fresh water.
  • Empty and rinse the drip tray.
  • Wipe down the steam wand and group head area with a damp microfiber cloth to remove any solution splatter.
  • Run a short blank shot (no coffee, no portafilter) to flush any remaining residue from the group head before brewing.

Keep Your Machine Cleaner Between Descaling Cycles

Descaling handles mineral scale, but daily grime requires its own routine. Consider adding these to your maintenance schedule:

  • Backflush weekly using espresso machine cleaning tablets and a blind basket
  • Rinse the portafilter and basket after every use
  • Replace the water tank filter every two months or as recommended

Troubleshooting & FAQ

What if the CLEAN ME light stays on after descaling?

If the light remains on after a completed cycle, the most common cause is that the cycle was interrupted before finishing. Start the descaling process again from the beginning with fresh solution. If the light persists after a full second cycle, consult Breville support — the sensor may need a manual reset.

Can I use citric acid instead of Breville's descaler?

Yes. Food-grade citric acid is a widely used alternative and is generally considered safe for espresso machines. Use approximately 1–2 tablespoons dissolved in 1.5 liters of water. Avoid industrial or non-food-grade citric acid, which may contain impurities.

What happens if I interrupt the cycle?

Stopping mid-cycle can leave acidic solution trapped in internal components. If you must stop, restart the full cycle from the beginning as soon as possible and complete it without interruption.

How often should I descale?

Every 2–3 months in hard water areas, or when the CLEAN ME light activates — whichever comes first. Setting your water hardness level correctly (see above) ensures the alert fires at the right time for your conditions.

Is descaling different from cleaning?

Yes. Descaling removes mineral scale from the internal boiler and water path. Cleaning (backflushing, tablet cleaning) removes coffee oils and residue from the group head and portafilter. Both are necessary for machine longevity.

Final Thoughts on How to Descale the Breville Barista Express

Following the descaling process correctly — using the right solution, completing all rinse cycles, and setting your water hardness accurately — protects your investment and keeps your espresso tasting as it should. The process is straightforward once you've done it once, and the 25–35 minutes it takes is a small commitment compared to the cost of repairs or a premature replacement.
